\midrule
Priti and Kumar (2022) \cite{ART01} & Critical review of AQI models (1960--2021). & Delimits the comparability problem across indices and the methodological heterogeneity of the field. & Does not address fuzzy inference or real-time monitoring as the focus of analysis. \\
Suman (2020) \cite{ART02} & Review of methods for interpreting air-quality status through AQI. & Reinforces the communicative role of AQI and its relationship to risk interpretation. & Does not analyze fuzzy-design decisions or intelligent monitoring architectures. \\
Murena (2004) \cite{PROB08} & Development and application of a pollution index in an urban context. & Brings the AQI discussion closer to a large urban-area implementation case. & Does not incorporate fuzzy inference or a comparative reading of real-time platforms. \\
Seibert et al. (2024) \cite{ART04} & Fuzzy air-quality classification. & Represents the line of work using fuzzy rules to classify air-quality states. & Focuses on a specific classification task rather than on a synthesis of design configurations. \\
Andrianto et al. (2024) \cite{ART06} & IoT monitoring with Mamdani fuzzy logic and ISPU/AQI-style interpretation. & Provides an antecedent close to the integration of sensors, index interpretation, and real-time operation. & Corresponds to a specific implementation and does not by itself allow comparison of methodological variants across the field. \\
Lingaraj et al. (2025) \cite{ART08} & Optimized WSN architecture for air monitoring in smart cities. & Introduces the dimension of distributed operation and network efficiency in urban deployments. & Prioritizes network architecture and does not articulate variables, fuzzy design, and system evaluation in a cross-cutting manner. \\
