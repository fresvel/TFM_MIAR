\section{Materials and Methods}

\subsection{Design of the Study}
This work corresponds to a \textit{Systematic Mapping Study} (SMS), whose objective is to provide a panoramic, structured, and quantitative view of the existing literature on the application of Artificial Intelligence models in medical images for the analysis, detection, and diagnosis of breast cancer. The SMS was developed following the methodological guidelines proposed by Petersen et al.\ (2008, 2015) and the adapted recommendations of Kitchenham for mapping studies in engineering and computer science.

Unlike a systematic review (SLR), whose purpose is to answer specific research questions, the SMS seeks to classify, quantify, and identify trends, gaps, and emerging areas in the scientific production of the analyzed domain.

\subsection{Objectives and Research Areas}
Because mapping studies are not structured using frameworks such as PICO/PICo, \textbf{research areas (Research Areas)} were defined to guide the categorization process:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{RA1: Imaging modalities used} (mammography, MRI, ultrasound, tomosynthesis, digital histology).
    \item \textbf{RA2: Applied AI techniques} (traditional ML, CNN, transformers, hybrid models, CAD).
    \item \textbf{RA3: Types of tasks addressed} (classification, segmentation, detection, localization, histopathological analysis).
    \item \textbf{RA4: Dataset characteristics and availability} (public, private, size, balance).
    \item \textbf{RA5: Trends and temporal evolution} (annual publication volume, emerging areas).
    \item \textbf{RA6: Field limitations and gaps} (external validation, generalization, data quality).
\end{itemize}

These areas constitute the basis of the taxonomy used in the analysis stage.

\subsection{Search Strategy}
The systematic search was carried out in the \textit{Scopus}, \textit{Web of Science}, \textit{ScienceDirect}, and \textit{PubMed} databases. Search strings were designed using combinations of general domain terms, including keywords related to artificial intelligence, deep learning models, and breast imaging modalities.

A generic example of a used string was:

\begin{quote}
(\textit{"breast cancer" OR "mammography" OR "breast imaging"}) AND  
(\textit{"artificial intelligence" OR "machine learning" OR "deep learning"})  
\end{quote}

Due to the exploratory nature of the SMS, strict restrictions on intervention, population, or context were not applied, as is customary in mapping studies.

\subsection{Study Selection}
Study selection was carried out in three stages, following the approach suggested by Petersen:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Identification}: removal of duplicates.
    \item \textbf{Initial screening}: review of title, abstract, and keywords to determine general relevance to the thematic area.
    \item \textbf{Extended assessment}: partial or full reading to confirm whether the study contained sufficient information to be classified in one of the defined areas.
\end{enumerate}

Strict exclusion criteria specific to SLRs (for example, specific metrics, external validity, experimental design type) were not applied, since the purpose of the SMS is to represent the breadth of the field.

\subsection{Classification Scheme}
Included studies were classified using a \textbf{taxonomic scheme} developed from the defined Research Areas (RA1--RA6). For each study, the following were recorded:

\begin{itemize}
    \item imaging modality;
    \item AI technique used;
    \item task type;
    \item dataset used;
    \item year of publication;
    \item source type (journal, conference);
    \item country and author affiliation;
    \item author keywords.
\end{itemize}

This classification made it possible to consolidate a global matrix of the research panorama.

\subsection{Data Extraction}
Data extraction was performed using a structured sheet based on Kitchenham's recommendations for SMS, complemented with variables used in bibliometric analyses (year, country, keywords, source, citations).

The data were systematized in tables and later integrated into a supplementary repository.

\subsection{Data Analysis}
The analysis was developed under two approaches:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Descriptive / quantitative analysis}:
    number of studies per year, distribution by imaging modality, techniques used, most used datasets, temporal evolution.
    \item \textbf{Bibliometric analysis (optional)}:
    performed with \texttt{bibliometrix} (R) and VOSviewer, including keyword co-occurrence, collaboration networks, and thematic evolution.
\end{enumerate}

Finally, the results were integrated into a conceptual map of the area, highlighting trends, research concentration, and existing gaps.
